The fundamental theorem of arithmetic in number theory states that every positive integer number greater than 1, x ∈ Z where x > 1, is a prime number or it is comprised of the unique product of prime numbers up to the order of the factors.

Example:

1250 = 21 × 54 = 2× 5× 5× 5× 5
1200 = 31× 52× 24 = 3× 5× 5× 2× 2× 2× 2

In other words:

  1. 1250 and 1200 can be represented as a product of primes
  2. there will always be a specific amount of primes in each product
