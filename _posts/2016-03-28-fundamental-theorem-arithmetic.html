The fundamental theorem of arithmetic in number theory states that every positive integer number greater than 1, x ∈ \Re{} where x > 1 is a prime number or it is comprised of the unique product of prime numbers up to the order of the factors.

'Example:'

1200 = 24 x 31 x 52 = 3x2x2x2x2
