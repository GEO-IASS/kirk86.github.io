The fundamental theorem of calculus can be broken down into 2 different parts:

  • 1st part: 1st fundamental theorem of calculus states that if \(f\) is continuous on the closed interval \([a, b]\) and \(F\) is the indefinite integral of \(f\) on $[a, b],$ then \(\int_{a}^{b} f(x) dx = F(b) - F(a)\)
